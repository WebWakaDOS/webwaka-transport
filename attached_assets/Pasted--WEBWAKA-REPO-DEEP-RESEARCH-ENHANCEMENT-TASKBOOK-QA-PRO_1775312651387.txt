# WEBWAKA REPO DEEP RESEARCH + ENHANCEMENT TASKBOOK + QA PROMPT FACTORY

You are the **expert researcher, platform analyst, enhancement architect, and QA planner** for this WebWaka repository.

Your mission is to perform a **very deep and thorough review** of this repository in the context of the full WebWaka ecosystem, then produce a **single comprehensive document** that contains:

1. a deep understanding of the repo’s current codebase, modules, capabilities, features, bugs, and limitations,
2. deep research into the internet for best practices, standards, and world-class benchmarks relevant to this repo’s vertical/modules/capabilities/features,
3. a synthesis of the repo reality and external best practices into the **top 20 enhancements** plus bugs to fix,
4. a conversion of those enhancements and bug fixes into detailed implementation tasks,
5. a detailed QA testing and verification plan for each task,
6. and for every task, a **copy-paste execution prompt pair**:
   - one prompt for implementation,
   - one prompt for QA/testing and bug fixing.

All of this must be written into a **document**, not posted as a chat response.

## Core mission rules

You must treat this repository as **part of the larger WebWaka ecosystem**, not as a standalone application.

That means:

- some capabilities in this repo may already be provided by other WebWaka repos,
- some missing pieces here may intentionally live elsewhere,
- some improvements require cross-repo awareness even if implementation is repo-local,
- some modules in this repo depend on shared contracts, core packages, or platform services from other repos,
- the repo documents and GitHub files already contain context you must read and understand deeply,
- the agent must not assume this repo contains everything needed to be complete on its own.

This repo is one component in a **multi-repo platform architecture**, and your analysis must reflect that clearly.

## Step 1 — Deep repo understanding

First, do a deep dive into this repo and build a very detailed understanding of:

- the repo structure,
- all modules and submodules,
- all capabilities and features,
- current flows and business logic,
- existing bugs,
- missing functionality,
- hidden constraints,
- config patterns,
- tests,
- migrations,
- docs,
- deployment setup,
- CI/CD setup,
- dependencies on other WebWaka repos,
- what is already implemented versus what still belongs elsewhere in the ecosystem.

You must consult the repo’s own documents, existing code, and internal structure until you have a comprehensive understanding of it.

## Step 2 — Deep internet research

Next, do deep external research into the internet to understand:

- best practices for this repo’s vertical,
- best practices for its modules and capabilities,
- best practices for its features and workflows,
- world-class standards and patterns used by leading products in this space,
- technical best practices relevant to the stack and architecture,
- UX, admin, automation, analytics, offline, mobile, AI, security, and operational standards relevant to the repo.

This research must be broad enough to surface strong enhancement opportunities, not just obvious fixes.

## Step 3 — Synthesis

Synthesize what exists inside the repo with what exists globally in best-in-class systems.

From this synthesis, identify:

- the top 20 enhancements,
- major bugs or gaps,
- missing capabilities,
- architecture improvements,
- UX improvements,
- admin and operations improvements,
- integrations or cross-cutting platform opportunities,
- mobile/PWA/offline opportunities,
- AI opportunities where relevant,
- performance and reliability improvements,
- documentation or CI/CD improvements.

Be comprehensive. Do not give only a few recommendations.

## Step 4 — Convert into taskbook

Convert all recommendations and bugs into **detailed implementation tasks**.

Each task must include:

- task title,
- objective,
- why it matters,
- repo scope,
- dependencies,
- prerequisites,
- impacted modules,
- likely files or areas to change,
- expected output,
- acceptance criteria,
- tests required,
- risks,
- governance documents to consult,
- important reminders.

Where a recommendation should be implemented in phases, split it into phase 1 and phase 2 tasks and clearly label them.

## Step 5 — Convert into QA plans

For every implementation task, create a matching QA plan that includes:

- what must be verified,
- what bugs to look for,
- what edge cases to test,
- what regressions to detect,
- what cross-module or cross-repo assumptions to verify,
- what deployment or config checks to perform,
- how to re-test after fixes,
- what “done” means for QA.

Each QA plan must be deep enough for an expert QA agent to execute immediately.

## Step 6 — Generate copy-paste prompts for each task

For every task, create two ready-to-copy execution prompts:

1. **Implementation prompt**
2. **QA prompt**

Each prompt must be extremely detailed and must include:

- the exact repo name,
- the exact objective,
- the relevant dependencies,
- the repository context,
- the ecosystem caveat that the repo is not standalone,
- the important reminders,
- the required deliverables,
- the acceptance criteria,
- the tests or verification criteria,
- the instruction to consult repo docs and governance docs before acting,
- the instruction to avoid drift and shortcuts.

These prompts must be copy-paste ready.
